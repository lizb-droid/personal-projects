## Wednesday Race Recovery Protocol

Wednesday is the highest weekly stress day. Formalize recovery:

- **Within 30 min post-race:** 25g protein + 30g carbs (mandatory — milk supply protection)
- **Thursday:** No running, no gym. Walk only.
- **Friday Upper B gate:** If Thursday soreness is >5/10 → drop 1 set from all main lifts + skip accessories. Upper B is sacrificial — protect Monday and Tuesday above all.

## Key Reasoning

**Lower A time fix:** Original ran 66–73 min. Cuts always fall on accessory work you actually need. Revised targets 54–58 min with warm-up buffer.

**GHD before Hip Thrust:** GHD primes the posterior chain neurally. Doing it after Hip Thrust 4×12 means erectors are already fatigued. Reversed = better primer, lower injury risk as loads increase.

**DB Pullover → Chest-Supported Row:** Original push:pull ratio ~3:1. Breastfeeding posture = chronic anterior shoulder load. Suppressed estrogen affects rotator cuff tendon health. Chest-supported row targets mid-traps and rhomboids — exactly the postural muscles weakened by nursing.

**Power movements first:** Peak power output window at 5 AM is ~20 minutes. Power quality degrades faster than strength quality — protect the window.

**No Phase 4 power add-ons:** Only 5 weeks between Cirque Alta (Jul 11) and Cirque Snowbird (Aug 15). Insufficient time to build and supercompensate. Attempting this risks carrying fatigue into Wk 17 taper. Maintain and sharpen what was built in Phase 2–3.

**Power add-on cutoff = Wk 10:** Last week of power add-ons is Wk 10 (peak week, Solitude Jun 24). Wk 11 is deload — no power work. Wks 12 + 17 are race weeks — no power work. Effectively: hit power hard this week (Wk 9) and Wk 10, then stop.

**Phase 4 Upper B volume reduction:** Upper B is already the sacrificial session. During Wks 14–16 maintenance, drop main lifts from 4 → 3 working sets. Accessories stay at 3×12. Goal is maintenance, not accumulation — protect recovery for Snowbird prep.

**Race-day calorie bump:** Lower A Mondays and all race days target 2,400–2,500 kcal (vs. standard 2,100–2,300). Training load + race effort = higher burn. Milk supply is the leading indicator — if it dips after a race week, calorie audit is step one.

**Intra-workout carbs (Phase 2+):** As training intensity increases, pre-workout snack may not sustain session quality AND protect milk supply. 20–30g fast carbs in last 15 min blunts the cortisol spike when glycogen drops. Directly protects prolactin levels.
