2. Sleep environment at 67°F or below. Post-peak-week core temp takes longer to drop; the body can't enter deep sleep until it does. Cool shower 90 min before bed if needed.

- Maintain circadian anchors: morning light within 30 min of wake, dim lights after 8 PM. Don't let bedtime drift later just because training volume is lower.

**The 2-nights-before rule:**
- July 9 (Thursday) is your most important sleep night of race week — not July 10.
- Race eve (July 10) sleep will likely be lighter due to adrenaline. This is normal and does not hurt performance if July 9 was solid. Do not catastrophize a short race-eve night.
- Protect July 9 like a non-negotiable.

**Overreach vs. normal taper fatigue:**
Normal: Fatigue that clearly improves as load drops over 72 hours. Gets better, directionally.
Overreach: Fatigue that plateaus or worsens despite reduced load. Watch the trajectory, not the absolute level.
Escalate if: Soreness >7/10 for 3+ days into taper, sleep quality <5/10 for 3+ consecutive nights, supply drop + fatigue together, resting HR still elevated by Thursday of taper week.

**Postpartum reality check:** Your recovery ceiling is slightly lower than your training log shows — some bandwidth goes to milk production, hormonal regulation, and infant wake-ups. Sleep practices in this window are load management, not optimization. Treat them accordingly.
